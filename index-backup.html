Executive Summary

الملخص التنفيذي

Comprehensive Overview of the Academic Research

This research presents a study integrating structural realism and information theories in international relations, examining how informational conditions can affect the effectiveness of structural constraint in achieving stability and peace.

The research is based on a central hypothesis that expectation convergence between states - resulting from improved information flow and Bayesian learning - can enhance the international structure's ability to regulate aggressive state behavior.

Main Academic Contribution

Development of an integrated theoretical framework linking structural realism (Waltz, Mearsheimer) and information theories (Fearon, Powell), focusing on the role of Bayesian learning in shaping strategic expectations and reducing conflict probabilities.

Research Structure

The research covers seven main chapters addressing theoretical foundations, research methodology, and applied case studies, focusing on historical cases that witnessed transformations in the informational environment and their implications for international behavior.

Practical Applications

The research provides applied analyses of contemporary conflicts such as US-China relations, Russia and NATO, Iran's nuclear file, and North Korea, with historical case studies including World War I, Cuban Missile Crisis, and the 2022 Ukraine War.

مستوى تقارب التوقعات

H1

Hypothesis One

الفرضية الأولى

Insufficient Informational Convergence

Mere expectation convergence between rival states is insufficient to achieve peace without effective structural constraint.

H2

Hypothesis Two

الفرضية الثانية

Determinants of Convergence Rate

The rate of expectation convergence is determined by information quality, communication channels, and parties' capacity for Bayesian learning.

H3

Hypothesis Three

الفرضية الثالثة

Structure-Information Interaction

Structural constraint effectiveness increases critically when synchronized with high expectation convergence rates.

H4

Hypothesis Four

الفرضية الرابعة

Critical Informational Threshold

There exists a critical informational threshold below which structural constraint fails to prevent war, and above which it becomes highly effective.
