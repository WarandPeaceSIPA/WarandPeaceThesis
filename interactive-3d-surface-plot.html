3D Visualization: The S × I × C Relationship

This interactive 3D surface plot visualizes the relationship between three critical variables in international conflict resolution: S - Structural Constraint, I - Information Quality, and C - Convergence.

Core Mathematical Model

C = αS + βI + γ(S × I)

Where: α = structural coefficient, β = information coefficient, γ = interaction coefficient

Critical Threshold Discovery

I ≥ 7 → Peace | I < 7 → War

Research shows that when Information Quality reaches 7 or above, peaceful outcomes become dominant regardless of structural constraints.

Variable Definitions

S - Structural Constraint

Measures institutional and systemic pressures on actors (0-10 scale)

I - Information Quality

Measures accuracy, completeness, and reliability of information (0-10 scale)

C - Convergence

Measures alignment of interests and expectations (0-10 scale)

Key Findings

Peace Cases (I ≥ 7)

Interaction Effect
