Analysis Overview

This quantitative analysis tests the proposed theoretical model on 28 historical cases spanning over a century (1910-2022). The model examines the relationship between three key variables:

  • S → Structural Constraint: The strength of structural constraints in the international system (1-10 scale)
  • I → Information Quality: The quality and accuracy of information available to decision-makers (1-10 scale)
  • C → Convergence Level: The degree of convergence between expectations and reality (Low/Medium/High)

Main Hypothesis: The effectiveness of structural constraints in preventing war depends on the quality of information available to decision-makers. When information quality is high, structural constraints are more effective in maintaining peace.

Variable Classification Criteria

Structural Constraint (S)

  • Weak (1-3): Minimal structural constraints, high freedom of action
  • Medium (4-6): Moderate constraints, balanced system
  • Strong (7-10): Significant constraints, limited freedom

Information Quality (I)

  • Low (1-3): Poor information, high uncertainty
  • Medium (4-6): Moderate information quality
  • High (7-10): Excellent information, low uncertainty

Convergence Level (C)

  • Low: Significant gap between expectations and reality
  • Medium: Moderate alignment
  • High: Strong convergence between expectations and outcomes

Correlation Analysis Results

War Cases (n=14)

Correlation between S and I: r = -0.42

Negative correlation: Higher structural constraints associated with lower information quality in war cases

Peace Cases (n=14)

Correlation between S and I: r = +0.68

Positive correlation: Higher structural constraints associated with better information quality in peace cases

Key Finding: The opposite correlation patterns between war and peace cases support the main hypothesis that information quality moderates the effectiveness of structural constraints.

Key Findings

Information Quality Matters[^>]*> Information Quality Matters Information Quality Matters>

High information quality (I ≥ 7) combined with strong structural constraints (S ≥ 7) consistently resulted in peace.

⚖️ Structural Constraints Alone Insufficient

Strong structural constraints without quality information failed to prevent war in multiple cases.

🔄 Convergence as Key Mediator

High convergence (C) between expectations and reality was present in all 14 peace cases.
